Our Programs

Long Island Head Start provides early childhood education and family support services from birth through age five.

Early Head Start (Birth–3)

Early Head Start serves pregnant women, infants, and toddlers. Services support early development, health, and strong parent-child relationships.

  • Center-based infant and toddler classrooms.
  • Home-based services with weekly visits.
  • Individualized learning plans for each child.

Head Start (3–5)

Head Start provides preschool services to help children enter kindergarten ready to learn.

  • Age-appropriate learning centers and activities.
  • Social-emotional development and school readiness skills.
  • Full-day and part-day options at different sites.

Family & Community Services

Family advocates work with parents and caregivers to set goals and connect with community resources.

  • Support with housing, employment, and education goals.
  • Referrals to community agencies and services.
  • Family engagement events and workshops.

Health & Nutrition

Health and nutrition services help children stay healthy so they are ready to learn.

  • Vision, dental, and hearing screenings.
  • Nutritious meals and snacks.
  • Support for medical and dental appointments.
